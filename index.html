Historic places in V&A waterfront
Clock Tower
  • Was the origin Port captain's office built in 1882
  • This was necessary to read the levels of the ocean and relay tide information to passing ships.
  • The Clock Tower was also known as the Dock Clock or Tide Gauge Tower;its original colour was white.
Alfred Basin
  • Up to 1860 Alfred was a dry area
  • The whole area was completely restored in the early 1990s,,
    and today it is a lively district packed with restaurants, bars and shops.
  • Construction began in 1860, when Prince Alfred,
    Queen Victoria's second son, tipped the first load of stone to start the building of the breakwater for Cape Town's harbour.
